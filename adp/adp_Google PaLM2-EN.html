The pyruvate is then converted into acetyl-CoA, which enters the citric acid cycle (also known as the Krebs cycle). In the citric acid cycle, a series of chemical reactions occur that result in the production of ATP, NADH, and FADH2 (flavin adenine dinucleotide). This gradient drives the synthesis of ATP through a process called oxidative phosphorylation. ADP plays a crucial role in oxidative phosphorylation. It binds to an enzyme called ATP synthase, which is located in the inner mitochondrial membrane. As protons flow down the gradient, they cause the ATP synthase to rotate, which leads to the formation of ATP from ADP and inorganic phosphate (Pi).

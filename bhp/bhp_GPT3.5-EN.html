BHP stands for Brake Horsepower. It is a unit of measurement used to quantify the power output of an engine. Brake horsepower refers to the power output of an engine without any additional power losses, such as those caused by the transmission, generator, or other auxiliary components. It is typically measured using a dynamometer, which applies a load to the engine and measures the power output at the engine's crankshaft.

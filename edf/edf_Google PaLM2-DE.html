**EDF** stands for **Earliest Deadline First**. It is a scheduling algorithm used in real-time systems to prioritize tasks based on their deadlines. The EDF algorithm assigns higher priority to tasks with earlier deadlines, ensuring that they are completed before their deadlines. This is in contrast to other scheduling algorithms such as First-Come First-Served (FCFS) or Round Robin, which do not consider deadlines when scheduling tasks. Here's how the EDF algorithm works: 1. **Maintain a queue of ready tasks, sorted by their deadlines.** The task with the earliest deadline is at the front of the queue. 2. **When a new task arrives, insert it into the queue based on its deadline.** If the new task has an earlier deadline than the task currently running, preempt the running task and start executing the new task. 3. **Continue executing tasks in the order of their deadlines.** If a task finishes before its deadline, remove it from the queue. If a task misses its deadline, it is considered a failure. The EDF algorithm is optimal in the sense that it minimizes the number of missed deadlines. However, it is also complex to implement and can be computationally expensive, especially for large systems with many tasks. EDF is commonly used in real-time systems where it is critical to meet deadlines, such as in avionics, medical devices, and industrial control systems.

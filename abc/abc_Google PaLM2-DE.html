This method is more accurate than traditional costing methods, which allocate costs based on direct labor or machine hours. For example, the cost of a car is driven by the activities that are required to design, engineer, manufacture, and sell the car. ABC costing can be used to improve profitability by identifying and eliminating non-value-added activities. Calculate the cost of each product or service by multiplying the cost of each activity by the number of times that activity is performed to produce the product or service.

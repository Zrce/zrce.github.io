DBS stands for Deep Brain Stimulation. It is a surgical procedure that involves implanting electrodes into specific areas of the brain to deliver electrical stimulation. DBS is used to treat a variety of neurological conditions, including Parkinson's disease, dystonia, essential tremor, and obsessive-compulsive disorder. During DBS surgery, the electrodes are placed in the brain under local anesthesia. The electrodes are then connected to a battery-powered device that is implanted under the skin in the chest. The device can be programmed to deliver electrical stimulation to the brain at specific intervals and intensities. DBS is a relatively safe and effective treatment for a variety of neurological conditions. However, it is important to note that DBS is not a cure for these conditions, and it may not be suitable for everyone.
